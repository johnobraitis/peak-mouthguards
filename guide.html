Step-by-step impression guide

How to take your impression

The whole process takes about 5 minutes. Read through this once before you start so nothing catches you off guard. Everything you need is already in your kit.

Before you begin

Set yourself up for a clean impression

Timing
Don't eat or drink for 30 minutes beforehand. Food residue and saliva can affect how the putty sets and seats against your teeth.
Setup
Work over a flat surface with good lighting. Have a timer ready on your phone. You'll need to move with purpose once the putty is mixed — you have about 60 seconds.
What's in the kit
Upper + lower impression trays, putty material (two parts), instruction card, prepaid return envelope. Check everything is there before you start.

The process

Step by step

01
Wash your hands

Wash and dry your hands thoroughly before handling the putty or trays. This keeps the material clean and prevents contamination that can affect the set.

02
Try the tray for size

Before mixing anything, place the upper tray in your mouth dry to check the fit. It should sit comfortably over your upper teeth without pressing into your gums. Do the same with the lower tray.

Good to know: The trays are designed to fit most adults. If one feels too wide or narrow, note it — the putty will still conform to your teeth once loaded.
03
Mix the putty — upper tray

Your kit contains two parts of putty material — a base and a catalyst. Take an equal amount of each (roughly the same size ball from each container). Knead them together firmly with your fingers until the color is completely uniform with no streaks. This takes about 30 seconds.

Important: Once you start mixing, you have approximately 60 seconds before the putty begins to set. Work quickly and confidently once mixing is complete.
04
Load the upper tray

Roll the mixed putty into a sausage shape and press it evenly into the upper tray. It should fill the tray fully — pushed into the front, sides, and back. Don't overfill to the point where it hangs over the edges.

Tip: Press your thumb along the center ridge of the tray to create a slight groove — this helps the putty seat evenly around your teeth.
05
Seat the upper tray

Stand in front of a mirror. Hold the loaded tray at both ends and insert it into your mouth, centering it over your upper teeth. Press it firmly and evenly upward until the tray is fully seated. Your upper teeth should sink into the putty.

Hold completely still for 2–3 minutes. Breathe through your nose. Do not talk, swallow excessively, or move your jaw — any movement will distort the impression.

Timer tip: Start your phone timer the moment you seat the tray. 2 minutes 30 seconds is the target hold time.
06
Remove the upper tray

After 2–3 minutes, remove the tray by pressing down on one side to break the seal, then pulling straight down and out in one smooth motion. Do not wiggle or twist — this can distort the impression you just captured.

Check the impression. You should see clear, sharp detail of each tooth — crowns, gaps, and contours. If anything looks unclear or incomplete, see the troubleshooting note below.

What a good impression looks like: Clear tooth outlines, no large air bubbles in the biting surface, putty extends slightly past the gum line on all sides.
07
Repeat for the lower tray

Mix a fresh set of putty for the lower tray using the same process — equal parts, knead until uniform, load the tray, seat it over your lower teeth, and hold for 2–3 minutes.

For the lower impression, press the tray firmly downward and hold still. The process is identical to the upper — just in the opposite direction.

08
Let the impressions set fully

Allow both impressions to sit undisturbed for at least 5 minutes after removal before packing them. The putty needs time to finish curing. Don't press on the impressions or store them in a warm place.

09
Pack and mail

Place both impression trays carefully in the prepaid return envelope included in your kit. The envelope is rigid enough to protect the impressions in transit. Seal it, affix it to the box, and drop it at any USPS location or mailbox.

Once it's in the mail, head to your order tracking page and confirm you've sent your impression back — this lets us know it's on the way.

Don't delay: The sooner you mail it back, the sooner your guard ships. Most customers who mail their impression the same day receive their guard fastest.

Troubleshooting

Common issues and fixes

Air bubbles in the impression

Usually caused by insufficient putty or not pressing the tray firmly enough. If bubbles appear over a tooth surface (not just the edges), contact us before sending it back.

Impression pulled loose when removing

This happens if you move your jaw during the hold or remove the tray too early. The impression may still be usable — we'll inspect it on arrival and let you know.

Putty didn't mix evenly

If you see streaks of color in the cured putty, the material wasn't fully mixed. Uneven mixing can affect how the guard fits. Contact us and we can arrange a replacement kit.

Not sure if it came out right

Email us a photo at hello@peakmouthguards.com with your order ID and we'll take a look before you send it in.
