Ordering

You're ordering a custom pro-fit mouthguard made from your own dental impression — not a boil-and-bite. When you place your order, we first ship you an impression kit. You take the mold at home (takes about 5 minutes), mail it back with the prepaid return envelope, and we fabricate your guard from your exact impression. It's then shipped back to you.

Everything is included: the kit, fabrication, carry case, and USPS shipping both ways.

Flat shipping rate of $8.95 covers USPS delivery both ways — kit to you and guard back to you.

We currently offer three colors: Clear, Yellow, and Blue. Select your preference when placing your order. If you have a custom logo add-on, your logo is applied regardless of color choice.

Taking your impression

It's easy. The kit includes upper and lower impression trays and professional-grade putty material. The whole process takes about 5 minutes, and the instruction card walks you through each step clearly. No prior experience required.

We also have a step-by-step guide on our impression instructions page if you want to preview the process before your kit arrives.

We quality-check every impression when it arrives. If something doesn't look right — bubbles, incomplete coverage, distortion — we'll reach out before fabricating and work out a solution. In most cases we can send a replacement kit.

Following the instructions carefully (especially the mixing and seating steps) gives you the best result on the first try.

Yes. The putty is BPA-free, non-toxic, and tasteless. It's the same type of material used in professional dental settings. Do not swallow it, but brief contact with teeth and gums is completely safe.

The guard

Boil-and-bite guards are generic — you soften them in hot water and bite down, hoping they conform to your teeth. The fit is approximate at best. They tend to be bulky, fall out during play, and need to be replaced frequently.

A Peak guard is fabricated from an actual impression of your teeth. The result is a guard that seats precisely, stays in place, and doesn't interfere with breathing or communication during play.

Any contact or combat sport: Lacrosse, Football, Hockey, Boxing, MMA, Basketball, Rugby, and more. If your sport involves physical contact or the risk of facial impact, a Peak guard is appropriate.

Yes. All Peak guards are made with BPA-free materials. We handcraft every guard in Irvine, CA using professional-grade materials.

We capture impressions of both your upper and lower teeth. Your finished guard covers the upper arch, which is standard for sports mouthguards and provides the most protection.

Yes — every finished guard ships with a protective carry case.

With regular care, a Peak guard should last a full season or more of regular training and competition. We recommend replacing it if it becomes visibly worn, torn, or if your teeth shift significantly (e.g. after orthodontic work).

Shipping

Here's the full timeline:

Kit dispatch: Within 24 hours of your order.
Kit delivery: Typically 2–3 business days via USPS.
Impression return: 2–3 business days after you mail it back.
Fabrication + dispatch: 3–5 business days after we receive your impression.

Most customers receive their finished guard within 10–14 days of ordering, depending on how quickly they take and return their impression.

A flat rate of $8.95 covers all USPS shipping — the kit to you and the finished guard back to you. The prepaid return envelope for your impression is also included in the kit at no extra cost.

Yes. Every order gets a tracking page at peakmouthguards.com/track. Your order ID is in your confirmation email. You can see exactly which stage your order is at — from kit dispatch through to guard delivered — at any time.

When your finished guard ships, you'll also receive a USPS tracking link by email.

Currently we ship within the United States only. International shipping may be available in the future — message us if you're interested and we'll let you know.

Custom logo

After placing your order, email your logo file to hello@peakmouthguards.com with your order ID in the subject line. We accept JPG or PNG files. Higher resolution images produce the best results.

Designs with clear contrast and bold shapes work best — team logos, initials, numbers, or simple graphics. Very fine detail or small text may not reproduce perfectly given the surface area of a mouthguard. When in doubt, send it over and we'll let you know how it'll look.

We don't currently offer digital proofs, but we review every design before fabricating. If anything looks off, we'll contact you before proceeding.
